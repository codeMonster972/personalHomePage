Favorite TV Shows

All the Light We Cannot See
(2023): This adaptation of Anthony Doerr's novel follows the intertwined lives of a blind French girl and a German boy during World War II. As they navigate the chaos of war, their paths converge in unexpected ways, highlighting the resilience of the human spirit amidst the darkness of conflict.
The Middle
(2009 - 2018): Set in the fictional town of Orson, Indiana, this family sitcom follows the daily struggles and triumphs of the working-class Heck family. From parenting woes to financial challenges, The Middle provides a relatable and humorous portrayal of middle-class life in America, anchored by its genuine characters and heartfelt storytelling.
Malcolm in the Middle
(2000 - 2006): A groundbreaking comedy series that revolves around the chaotic yet endearing life of Malcolm, a highly intelligent teenager, and his eccentric family. With its sharp humor and unique narrative style, the show offers a hilarious and heartwarming glimpse into the challenges of growing up in a dysfunctional yet loving household.
Scrubs
(2001 - 2010): Set in the fictional Sacred Heart Teaching Hospital, this medical comedy-drama follows the personal and professional lives of the quirky staff, led by the insightful yet whimsical Dr. John "J.D." Dorian. Through its blend of humor, drama, and poignant moments, Scrubs provides a heartfelt exploration of friendship, love, and the realities of working in healthcare.
Community
(2009 - 2015): This offbeat sitcom revolves around a diverse group of students at Greendale Community College who form an unlikely bond while navigating the ups and downs of higher education. Fueled by its meta-humor, clever storytelling, and pop culture references, Community offers a refreshingly unconventional take on the college experience.
